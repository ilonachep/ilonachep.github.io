Welcome to my Interactive resume! I’m Ilona and I’m an avid web developer. Always learning. Always enquiring.

My professional life could be easily encompassed in a three-point timeline: media - law - programming. In actuality, there are more to it.

The last few years have become the most transformative years for me. Having extensive work background in media environment (I worked out content grids, planned and acquired content for worldwide viewers for Sony Pictures, Viasat Broadcasting UK, Turner, A+E Networks and others, I’ll keep them all in my linkedIn history:) at some point there was no further progression and I started to realise a growing craving to meet more challenges and actively expand knowledge on areas of interest.

These aspects led me to take up a law degree. During the course in my third year I took part in an international strategic forum on IP, digital economy and technologies domino affect in legal industry and their eventual push-through into this rather conservative and lagging field. The topics of data, effective mechanisms for protection of program code and idea, and few other events really knocked my socks off. They planted a seed of vivid interest and for the first time I consciously asked myself the whole array of questions: What is technology? What’s behind all those beautiful interfaces? How’s all this running? Though they were just seeds, initial questions, sheer curiosity that could have vanished under the pressures of other responsibilities, but they did not due to one infamous global event.

Despite it all, Covid-wide restrictions allowed a wonderful opportunity to start acting on that initial interest - not just ask questions but look for answers, grasp new knowledge, seek understanding in a stack of existing technologies. I’ve eagerly hopped on a bandwagon of The Complete 2022 Web Development Bootcamp at London App Brewery with Dr. Angela Yu and Responsive Web Design at freeCodeCamp.org which I’ve found both fascinating.

To sum it up, the most I like about this industry is that it provides tools and frameworks to bring order into chaos, and its adaptive nature when better instruments are needed to be used to meet challenges of a tomorrow.
